Project Proposal¶

The goal of the study is to predict the risk of death due to heart failure caused by cardiovascular diseases for patients based on clinical features and high-risk behavior.

The dataset I used contains the medical records of 299 heart failure patients collected at the Faisalabad Institute of Cardiology and at the Allied Hospital in Faisalabad (Punjab, Pakistan), during AprilDecember 2015, sourced from this published paper: https://bmcmedinformdecismak.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s12911-020-1023-5. Factors considered are age, sex, high blood pressure, smoking, diabetes, ejection fraction, creatinine phosphokinase, serum creatinine, serum sodium, and time.

The major analysis methods I will use are machine learning (Chapter 31) and cross-validation (Chapter 29). In this binary classification problem, the prediction will be made on the target variable DEATH EVENT (if the patient died during the follow-up period) by a variety of classification models and cross-validation to test and compare the models' performance to give the best prediction (such as random forest, logistic regression, and K-nearest neighbors). Besides, some data preprocessing methods, such as SMOTE and feature scaling, will be applied.

Introduction¶

The topic I studied is the prediction for heart failure (mortality) caused by cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) for patients through some clinical features and high-risk behavior. According to the American Heart Association, CVDs are the leading cause of death worldwide, accounting for 17.9 million deaths each year. Therefore, it is important to study and understand the disease pattern and influential factors in order to prevent and treat it effectively.

The dataset I used contains the medical records of 299 heart failure patients collected at the Faisalabad Institute of Cardiology and at the Allied Hospital in Faisalabad (Punjab, Pakistan), during AprilDecember 2015, sourced from this published paper: https://bmcmedinformdecismak.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s12911-020-1023-5 (Chicco and Jurman, 2020). Factors considered are age (age of the patient), sex (woman or man), high blood pressure (if a patient has hypertension), smoking (if the patient smokes), diabetes (if the patient has diabetes), ejection fraction (percentage of blood leaving), creatinine phosphokinase (level of the CPK enzyme in the blood), serum creatinine (level of creatinine in the blood), serum sodium (level of sodium in the blood), and time (follow-up period). Among 299 patients, 105 of whom were women and 194 were men, with ages ranging from 40 to 95 years old. All of the patients had left ventricular systolic dysfunction and had previously experienced heart failure, putting them in NYHA classes III or IV.

Exploratory data analysis was performed in the first step. There are no missing values for all variables in this dataset, and they are all numeric. In total, there are 299 observations and 13 variables measured. Table 1 can be referred to in detail for the summary statistics, containing the count, mean, standard deviation, minimum, maximum, and quartile values for each numeric column in the original data frame. Correlation was also tested for all variables. Based on Figure 2 and Table 2, we can see that the first three pairs with the highest correlation coefficients are time and DEATH_EVENT (0.819231), sex and smoking (0.725202), serum_creatinine and DEATH_EVENT (0.540025), which will be used as references for important features.

In the data preprocessing step, SMOTE and feature scaling were used. SMOTE (Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique) was used for balancing class distributions in the dataset. It is a type of over-sampling. In this study, imbalanced data was identified (Figure 1) and SMOTE was used to increase the number of samples in the minority class by creating synthetic samples to balance the class distribution and improve the model performance (Chawla et al, 2002). Feature scaling was applied to standardize the features of a dataset by scaling them to have a mean of 0 and a standard deviation of 1. This scaling can help models to perform well because many algorithms assume that the features of a dataset are normally distributed and have the same scale (LeCun et al, 1998).

For solving the classification problem in this study, random forest, logistic regression, and K-nearest neighbors were conducted and compared. Random forest is a supervised learning algorithm used for classification and regression. It is an ensemble method that combines the predictions of multiple individual models (decision trees) to make a final prediction, which allows it to capture complex patterns in the data (Breiman, 2001). SHAP (Shapley Additive Explanations) was used for evaluating the performance of the random forest. SHAP uses Shapley values to explain the contribution of each feature to the overall prediction of a model. This helps to identify the most important features in the model and to understand how the model is making predictions (Lundberg and Lee, 2017). K-nearest neighbors (KNN) is a supervised learning algorithm and a non-parametric method, which means that it does not make assumptions about the underlying data distribution. In KNN, a given data point is assigned the label of the majority of its "nearest neighbors," which are data points in the training set that are closest to it in terms of a distance metric such as Euclidean distance (Cover and Hart, 1967). Logistic regression is a supervised learning algorithm used for classification. It is a linear model that is used to predict the probability of a binary outcome (David Cox, 1958). In logistic regression, the receiver operating characteristic (ROC) curve is a graphical representation of the relationship between the true positive rate and the false positive rate of a classifier, and the area under the curve (AUC) is used as a tool for evaluating the performance (DeLong et al, 1988).

Cross validation is a technique that allows us to alleviate the overtraining problem and the apparent error from a larger population (Irizarry, 2014). In this study, K-fold cross-validation is used to evaluate the performance of every model. The dataset is divided into K (K=10 here) equal-sized folds, and the model is trained and evaluated K times, with each fold being used as the test set once and the remaining folds being used as the training set. This allows the model to be trained and tested on different subsets of the data, which helps to prevent overfitting and to produce a more accurate estimate of the model's performance (Stone, 1974).

Results¶

In the machine learning analysis part, the data set was split into training (80%) and testing sets (20%). The training set will be used to train a machine learning model, while the testing set will be used to evaluate the performance of the model. The target variable we try to predict is set to 'DEATH_EVENT.' The random_state parameter is set to 42, which is used to specify the random seed for the data split, ensuring that the same split is obtained every time the code is run.

Because the original data is imbalanced (Figure 1), SMOTE was used to balance the data in the training set. After SMOTE, length of oversampled data is 336 with same death and survival number of 168 and proportion of death and survival in oversampled data are both equal 0.5 (Table 3). Then the test_size argument specifies the proportion of the data that should be allocated to the testing set (in this case, 30%) for better performance later. After that, feature scaling was performed to standardize the range of independent variables or features and scales the data so that it has a mean of 0 and a standard deviation of 1 for both train and test sets. In total, there are 235 observations with 12 features in train set and 101 observations with 12 features in test set for modeling part.

Random forest was applied first. The code first creates an instance of the RandomForestClassifier class from the sklearn.ensemble module, with three hyperparameters specified: max_depth, n_estimators, and random_state. The max_depth hyperparameter specifies the maximum depth of the tree, which determines how much the tree can grow before it stops splitting. The n_estimators hyperparameter specifies the number of trees in the forest. The numbers of the three are the best results after parameter tuning using gridsearch. The score returned the model's accuracy (0.9307), which is the proportion of correct predictions it made on the testing data. Then, K-fold (K=10 here) cross-validation was done (Table 4) for random forest. The scoring parameter in this code specifies that the model should be evaluated using the "accuracy", "precision", "recall", and "f1" metrics. Accuracy is the proportion of correct predictions made by the model, precision is the proportion of correct positive predictions, recall is the proportion of actual positive cases that were correctly predicted, and F1 is the harmonic mean of precision and recall. The scores for each metric and each fold were displayed in Table 4, allowing the user to see how the model performed on each fold and overall. Also, from the Table 5, we can get average that test_accuracy is 0.906695, test_precision is 0.896593, test_recall is 0.927473, and test_f1 is 0.908890. This is the best model in this study and thus performance metrics of predication from random forest. From Table 6, we can see the precision for survival patients and recall for died patients are pretty high in 96% accuracy, which indicates that the model is very good at making accurate predictions about which patients will survive and which patients will die.

Based on the great performance, important features were obtained from the model result by feature_importance and SHAP. The feature importance in descending order (Figure 3 and Figure 4) and showed that "time", "serum creatinine", and "ejection fraction" are the top 3 features and much higher than the rest and have the biggest average impact on model output magnitude. Time is the follow up period at Allied Hospital. This result is reasonable that longer hospital time means more serious patients' health conditions, which is the summarizing and direct cause of the death. Remarkably, through the correlation test, there is very weak correlation between age and time (0.5129), which helps to exclude the assumption that age played bigger role than time and they have collinearity. Follwed by time, serum creatinine, level of creatinine in the blood, which is a key factor for renal dysfunction and was proven as an important clinical feature, especially in extreme serious situations(Chicco and Jurman, 2020). The third important factor is ejection fraction, percentage of blood leaving, which is the other supporting tool for physicians when predicting if a heart failure patient will survive or not.

Although good performance models had already been achieved, logistic regression and KNN were also applied for model interpretation and comparsion. From Table 7, the overall accuracy of logistic regression prediction is 86.1386, with a relatively high recall for the dead (0.90) and low recall for survival (0.82). Then ROC curve was performed for the relationship between the false positive rate and true positive rate, and AUC was calculated for this model(0.8842). This indicates that the model is performing well and has a high ability to distinguish between the positive and negative classes. Then, a detailed explanation for each coefficient was obtained from the logistic regression model interpretation (Table 8). Akaike information criterion (AIC) is 235.5385, which is a measure of how well the model is able to predict the outcome based on the available data and indicates that the model is not performing particularly well here. The log-likelihood ratio (LLR) p-value is a measure of the significance of the predictor variables in a logistic regression model. 3.5866e-48 indicates that the predictor variables in the model are highly significant and have a strong effect on the outcome. We can also read which variables are statistically significant (P-value is smaller than 0.05) in the model and whether they are positive or negative influence the outcome by the sign of the coefficient. Remarkably, time, serum_creatinineis, and ejection_fraction were significant associates with death on negative and positive sides (P-value < 0.0001), which also supported and provided visualized interpretation with the random forest results. Finally, cross-validation (10 fold) was made for logistic regression, and detailed results for all scores are in Table 9 for each fold. And average scores are listed below, test_accuracy (0.855616), test_precision (0.844980), test_recall (0.871970), and test_f1 (0.857047).

In the end, the K Neighbors Classifier method was applied to compare the models' results. After parameter tuning, the model got the best performance when k equals 7, which is 0.8218 for accuracy in total. The model performed equally for each stratum (Table 11) for 0.82, suggesting an excellent prediction for survival and death events. Then, ten-fold cross-validation was done for KNN. Detailed results for all scores are in Table 12 for each fold. We can see the variance and fluctuation in these scores are more extensive than other models above and thus showed relatively low performance. And average scores are listed below and in Table 13, test_accuracy (0.796377), test_precision (0.804993), test_recall (0.806061), and test_f1 (0.797702).

Conclusion¶

The target question for this study is to predict the risk of death due to heart failure caused by cardiovascular diseases for patients based on clinical features and high-risk behavior. In the data preprocessing step, SMOTE method was used for sample balancing, and feature scaling was performed for standardization. Then, three models were applied and compared for performance in the classification part. Among them, random forest is the best model with high accuracy (0.9307) and recall (0.9275), and 10-fold cross-validation accuracy is 0.9067. Based on the important features and SHAP values obtained from it, the top three key factors are "time," "serum creatinine," and "ejection fraction," which were interpreted by both statistics in model fitting and by clinical subject matter knowledge for the high risk of heart failure and proven to be noticed both in the health status of patients and future research studies.

Logistic regression was applied for better interpretation (accuracy score 0.86). AUC was calculated for logistic regression ROC (0.8842) and small P-values (< 0.0001) for all three top features helped to explain the significance statistically. 10-fold cross-validation accuracy is 0.86. Additional models considered were KNN (K=7 optimally) with 0.82 accuracies, and 10-fold cross-validation accuracy for KNN is 0.79.

Yes, this analysis is successful with high-accuracy models after different tries and the same important features results as the published paper (Chicco & Jurman, 2020) from this dataset. If I have more time, I will select only some of the most important features, such as topic 3, to fit the model again to see if I can get more accurate mortality predictions than using the original dataset features in its entirety.

def get_redundant_pairs(df):
    '''Get diagonal and lower triangular pairs of correlation matrix'''
    pairs_to_drop = set()
    cols = df.columns
    for i in range(0, df.shape[1]):
        for j in range(0, i+1):
            pairs_to_drop.add((cols[i], cols[j]))
    return pairs_to_drop

def get_top_abs_correlations(df, n=5):
    au_corr = df.corr().abs().unstack()
    labels_to_drop = get_redundant_pairs(df)
    au_corr = au_corr.drop(labels=labels_to_drop).sort_values(ascending=False)
    return au_corr[0:n]
